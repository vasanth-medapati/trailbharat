Trail hazards, seasonal warnings, emergency contacts, medical info
⚠ Current Trail Alerts — Gujarat
🌡 38–42°C
Extreme Heat Warning — April–June
Avoid trail activity 11am–4pm. Carry min 2L water. Risk of dehydration and heat stroke. Best trekking hours: 6–10am or post 5pm.
🌧 Monsoon
Flash Flood Risk — Jul–Sep
Nalsarovar and Sabarmati riverbanks flood rapidly. Check IMD alerts before trekking. Avoid stepwells during heavy rain — steps get extremely slippery.
✅ Oct–Mar
Optimal Trekking Season
Perfect weather 18–28°C. All trails accessible. Nalsarovar best for birds Nov–Feb. Carry light jacket for early mornings.
Trail-Specific Hazards
💧
Adalaj Vav — Wet Steps
Stone steps are polished and extremely slippery when wet. Hold railings. Avoid during or after rain. No railing on lowest level.
🌊
Sabarmati Riverfront — Flash Floods
River level can rise rapidly during monsoon. Stay on elevated promenade paths. Never descend to riverbed during July–September.
🐍
Indroda Park — Snakes
Gujarat has 60+ snake species. Stick to marked paths. Avoid tall grass. Wear closed shoes. Snake bite: call 108 immediately.
☀️
Sun Exposure — All Sites
UV index extreme March–October. Use SPF 50+, hat, and sunglasses. Sandstone at Akshardham reflects heat — stay hydrated.
🦟
Mosquitoes — Nalsarovar & Riverfront
Monsoon and post-monsoon risk. Use repellent. Risk of dengue and malaria. Wear full-length clothing during dawn/dusk.
Essential Safety Checklist
💧
Water — minimum 1.5L per person
Dehydration risk is extreme in Gujarat heat. Refill at sites or carry extra.
📱
Charged phone + power bank
Keep phone above 30% before heading out. Save offline maps before leaving WiFi.
👟
Closed-toe shoes for stepwells & nature parks
Open sandals are unsafe on wet stone and uneven terrain.
🆔
ID card + emergency contact written down
In case of emergency and phone is dead or lost.
🩹
Basic first aid — antiseptic, bandage, ORS
ORS sachets critical for heat exhaustion on long treks.
Emergency Action Guide
🩸 Snake Bite
1. Don't panic — stay still. 2. Immobilise the bitten limb. 3. Do NOT suck the venom, cut or apply tourniquet. 4. Call 108 immediately. 5. Keep victim calm and below heart level. Anti-venom available at Civil Hospital Ahmedabad: 079-2325-0000
🌡 Heat Stroke
1. Move to shade immediately. 2. Pour cool water on skin/clothes. 3. Fan vigorously. 4. Give ORS or plain water if conscious. 5. Call 108. Do NOT give aspirin or paracetamol for heat stroke — it worsens condition.
🌊 Drowning / Flash Flood
1. Never enter fast-moving water. 2. Call 108 and local fire brigade (101). 3. If stuck, move to highest ground. 4. Do not try to cross flooded paths — 6 inches of moving water can knock you down.
